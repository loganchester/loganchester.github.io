NEW ZEALAND

These photos offer just a small sample of my first roadtrip in New Zealand! I travelled with my friend Taylor who is also taking a semester abroad at the University of Otago. Our route looked something like this: Dunedin -> Oamaru -> Timaru -> Mt Cook National Park -> Wanaka -> Mt Aspiring National Park -> Queenstown -> Dunedin. The trip took us six days to complete using our trusty rental Toyota Yaris, Penelope.


While in Mt Cook National Park, we summitted Mt Ollivier. This was the first major climb that Sir Edmund Hillary completed in his legendary career as the most recognizable mountaineer in New Zealand's history! We also visited the Tasman Glacier and hiked along the popular Hooker Valley track. Next, Taylor and I drove to the beautiful lakeside town of Wanaka. We went kayaking on Lake Wanaka and had some amazing ice cream before driving to our campsite in Mt Aspiring National Park. From here, we visited the Blue Pools and hiked Mt Iron. Finally, on our way back to Dunedin, we made a quick stop to check out Queenstown.


Take a look at some of my favourite pictures from this trip below!



The next section includes pictures of my temporary hometown, Dunedin, and some weekend adventures that my friends and I have enjoyed. I think that the grass in Central Otago may just have been the most comfortable bed I have slept in while in New Zealand!



Before the semester started, I joined the Otago University Tramping Club so that I could explore the south island while going on some amazing hikes! My first trip with this club was to Fiordland National Park. My group climbed Mt. Burns and continued on to Green Lake where we stayed in a small hut and stargazed before making our way back to Dunedin. We had some (too) friendly Kea's join our trip to the summit of Mt. Burns!



The COVID-19 pandemic knows no bounds. It has affected the entire planet in a way that we just didn't see coming, and I am no exception. My time in New Zealand was cut short due to the Canadian Government's call for all travellers to return home. Although I am safely back in Victoria, I will never forget the incredible memories that I had on my (brief) exchange. In particular, I miss my incredible roommates so much. This section is a tribute to them. See you guys soon!
